# EduFlow Broad Curriculum Pack

Updated: 2026-06-22

## Curriculum Framework

This curriculum pack is designed for EduFlow as 11 standalone Secondary+ courses with a Kenya + global lens. It is suitable for schools, training institutions, independent learners, educator professional development, and career-readiness programs.

Default structure for every course:

- Duration: 6 weeks
- Pace: 3 lessons per week, about 2 to 4 hours per week
- Lessons: 18 lessons per course
- Assessments: 1 weekly quiz, 1 weekly practical activity, and 1 final assignment or project
- Certificate rule: certificate awarded after completing all lessons, scoring at least 70% average across weekly quizzes, and submitting the final project
- Delivery fit: each course maps to EduFlow fields for title, description, target learners, prerequisites, learning outcomes, modules, lessons, resources, quizzes, assignments, references, and certificate criteria

Academic note:
This pack is CBC-aware through Kenya's Basic Education Curriculum Framework, but it is not an official KICD syllabus, exam-preparation guarantee, legal advice, financial advice, medical advice, or engineering safety certification. Schools should align pacing and assessments with their own regulatory requirements.

## Course 1: Mathematics Foundations and Applied Problem Solving

Description: A practical mathematics course that strengthens number sense, algebra, geometry, functions, probability, statistics, and modeling for school, work, coding, science, and finance.

Target learners: Secondary+ learners, bridging students, adults returning to study, and learners preparing for STEM or business pathways.

Prerequisites: Basic arithmetic, fractions, decimals, and willingness to practice problem solving.

Learning outcomes:
- Use numbers, ratios, percentages, and units accurately in real situations.
- Solve linear, quadratic, and simultaneous-equation problems.
- Interpret graphs, functions, tables, and data summaries.
- Apply geometry, trigonometry, probability, and statistics to practical decisions.
- Build mathematical models for finance, science, geography, and coding contexts.

Required resources:
- Scientific calculator or calculator app

- EduFlow worksheet pack: formulas, graphing, statistics, and modeling templates
- OpenStax Algebra and Trigonometry 2e

## Cross-Course Assessment Model

Weekly quizzes:
- 8 to 12 questions per week
- Mix of multiple choice, short answer, applied calculation, diagram interpretation, and scenario judgment
- Automated feedback should explain why answers are correct or incorrect

Weekly practical activities:
- Designed for low-cost implementation
- Should accept document, photo, spreadsheet, code, chart, map, reflection, or short video evidence
- Should include a short rubric with accuracy, evidence, communication, and reflection

Final projects:
- Mathematics: applied model and explanation
- Biology: biology action brief
- Chemistry: chemistry safety and sustainability case study
- Physics: physics explanation of a technology or environmental issue
- Basic Computer 101: digital productivity portfolio
- Code Learning: small app, script, or data-backed dashboard
- AI Learning: AI-use portfolio with prompt log and risk review
- History and Geopolitics: geopolitical briefing
- Geography: local geographic inquiry report
- Law: rights-and-responsibilities guide
- Finance: personal finance or microbusiness plan

Certificate levels:
- Completion certificate: all lessons complete, final project submitted, quiz average 70% or higher
- Merit certificate: quiz average 80% or higher plus final project rubric 80% or higher
- Distinction certificate: quiz average 90% or higher plus final project rubric 90% or higher and strong evidence/reflection
